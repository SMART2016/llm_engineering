Open Source Vs Open Weight Models
	Open-source models and open-weight models are two distinct ways AI models are made available, each with unique transparency, flexibility, and control implications for users and developers[1][3][6].

### Definition and Key Differences

- **Open-Source Models**: These release all relevant assets needed to understand, modify, and reproduce the modelâ€”including training code, model architecture, training methodology, optimization scripts, and ideally the training dataset (or full documentation of how data was curated)[1][3][5]. This enables complete transparency and lets anyone audit, adapt, retrain, or fully replicate the model[1][3].
- **Open-Weight Models**: These release only the final trained parameters (weights and biases) of the AI model[1][2][3]. Users can run inference or fine-tune, but lack access to the original training pipeline, code, or often the dataset, meaning full auditability or reproduction isn't possible[1][3][5].

### Practical Implications

- Open-weight models allow fast deployment and community adaptation (fine-tuning, repurposing) but limit transparency and thorough auditability. Users can leverage advanced model capabilities but are restricted from fully understanding or modifying the underlying logic[1][4][5].
- Open-source models grant full control, transparency, and auditability, letting the community improve, understand, and analyze both the technology and its potential impacts[1][6].

### Summary Table

| Feature                | Open Weights    | Open Source      |
|------------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Model parameters       | Available [1] | Available [3]   |
| Training code          | Not shared [1][3] | Fully shared [3] |
| Training dataset       | Not disclosed [3] | Ideally shared or fully documented [3][5] |
| Full reproducibility   | Limited [1] | Achievable [3] |
| Transparency           | Partial [1] | Full [3]        |
| Auditability           | Limited [1] | Complete [3]    |

### Conclusion

Open weights make powerful AI accessible, but only open-source models support complete transparency, reproducibility, and community-driven advancement[1][3][4][6]. Choosing between these options impacts your level of control, ability to customize, and the reliability of audits for bias, safety, and other factors.
